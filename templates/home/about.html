Our History

Founded mid-20th century

Kalabu Mission School began as a small community-run school drawing student from the Kalabu settlement and surrounding suburbs (Samabula and Laqere) The school was located near Laqere Quarry from its early days with the help of the missionaries running the school. The quarries at Laqere Nasinu began its operation in the early 1930s which prompted the shifting of Kalabu Mission School to Kalabu Village.

1930s – 1940s

The school was built on a piece of land known as Naitiqatiqa where it was being operated by Missionaries, the land is located at Kalabu Village which is now the residence of former school Manager Mr. Asaeli Malewa of Kalabu Village.This period the Laqere Bridge was a trading center for coastal villages in the Rewa Delta.

Early 1950s

In the early 1950s the school was shifted to its present site. Kalabu Mission School was led by Missionaries M & Mrs. Jack Hazel Easton.

1960s

It was a fully-fledged school led by Mr. Joni Tabuya from Tilliva,, Nakasaleka Kadavu In 1960 the school was formalized as KALABU FIJIAN SCHOOL and given a registration number. In 1812 From its early days, Kalabu Primary was closely tied to the community. In 1966, Ratu David Toganivalu spoke at the school during the establishment of a Fijian Association branch, highlighting community involvement The school served a diverse student body, including Fijian and part-Chinese children from multiple suburbs.

1969 – 1970s

By 1969, the school hosted about 309 students, housed across two aging wooden classrooms, a few dilapidated traditional bures, and a tin shack. Conditions were poor with issues such as exposed walls, leaking roofs, and muddy access paths Governance rested with a local management committee that had lapsed in activity, leading to structural deterioration. Appeals for government support in 1959 and 1965 were rejected until a small grant of FJ $2,200 was finally provided in 1969.

1970s – 1980s

From the period of 1970s to 1980s the school developed much further by expanding its operation. New Building was built and more plans to develop the school was implemented.

1990s – 2000s

Kalabu Fijian School underwent a significant phase of development that helped shape the modern face of the school today. This decade marked major improvements in infrastructure to accommodate a growing student population and to enhance the quality of education. Key Developments: • ✅ Double-Story Building Completed: A major milestone was the construction and completion of a double-story classroom block. This expansion provided additional classrooms, helping to ease overcrowding and allowing for better learning environments. • ✅ Multi-Purpose Hall Built: A multi-purpose hall was also constructed during this period. The hall became a central hub for school activities, including assemblies, cultural events, meetings, and indoor sports. These developments reflected the strong support from the community, the school management, and relevant government ministries, all working together to improve the standard of education in the Kalabu area.

LATE 2000 to Date

The school’s name was changed to KALABU PRIMARY SCHOOL and a new ECE was established in 2024 known as KALABU ECE.

Expansion & Development

As enrolment climbed—now reaching approximately 700 primary students and 200 secondary students—the need for expanded facilities became a priority In recent years, significant upgrades were made under Japan’s Grassroots Human Security Projects. In November 2024, a new kindergarten classroom and ablution block were officially opened, funded by a FJ $382,000 donation from the Japanese government This initiative reflects a transformation from its humble beginnings—improving student health, hygiene, and early learning infrastructure.

PRESENT STAGE

The school is now enrolling 650 students and is being assisted by 20 teachers from humble beginnings Kalabu Primary School acknowledges the past teachers and Missionaries that have contributed to the establishment of the school. The school is fortunate to have enrolled thousands of students including educating past and present leaders including a former Prime Minister of Fiji the late Mr. Laisenia Qarase.
